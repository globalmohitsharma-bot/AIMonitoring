Program 01

Education Support Program

We believe every child deserves the chance to learn — and no family should have to choose between food and their child's education.

The Education Support Program provides comprehensive assistance to students from economically disadvantaged households in Haridwar and surrounding villages. Through regular distribution drives, we supply school bags, textbooks, notebooks, stationery, uniforms, and shoes — everything a child needs to attend and succeed in school.

Beyond materials, we also provide financial aid to families facing extreme hardship, ensuring children can continue their education without interruption due to fees or exam costs.

What We Provide

  • School bags, textbooks, and notebooks
  • Stationery sets and drawing supplies
  • Uniforms and school shoes
  • Financial aid for fees and examinations
  • Guidance and mentorship sessions

Program 02

Health & Wellness Camps

Healthcare is a fundamental right — yet millions in rural India have never seen a doctor. We bring qualified medical professionals directly to communities.

Our Health & Wellness Camps are organized regularly across Haridwar district and neighboring villages. Each camp includes general medical check-ups, blood pressure and sugar screening, eye examinations, and free prescription glasses for those with vision impairments.

We also run hygiene and sanitation awareness sessions, mother and child health education, and distribute free medicines to those who cannot afford them.

Camp Services

  • General medical examination and consultation
  • Eye testing with free prescription glasses
  • Blood pressure, diabetes, and hemoglobin screening
  • Free medicine distribution
  • Maternal and child health support
  • Health and hygiene awareness sessions

Program 03

Food & Essentials Distribution

No one should go to sleep hungry. Our food distribution drives reach those who fall through every other safety net.

Through regular monthly drives, we distribute ration kits, cooked meals, and essential supplies to homeless individuals, widowed women, daily-wage labourers, and families facing economic hardship in Haridwar and nearby areas.

Each drive is organized by our dedicated volunteer teams, who collect, sort, package, and personally distribute supplies — ensuring maximum dignity in every interaction.

What We Distribute

  • Monthly ration kits (rice, dal, oil, spices)
  • Cooked meals during festivals and emergencies
  • Clothing and warm blankets in winter
  • Hygiene products (soap, toothpaste, sanitary items)
  • Baby food and infant supplies

Program 04

Support for Elderly & Widows

The elderly and widowed are often invisible to society. We ensure they are seen, respected, and cared for with the dignity they deserve.

Many senior citizens and widows in our communities live alone, without family support or pension income. Our dedicated program reaches out to these individuals through regular home visits, community gatherings, and essential supply distributions.

We also organize festive celebrations and community events specifically for elders — because belonging to a community is as essential as food and medicine.

Our Support Includes

  • Monthly essential supply delivery to homes
  • Medical support and clinic visits
  • Community gathering and festival celebrations
  • Emotional support and regular check-ins
  • Help with government scheme enrollment

Program 05

Rural Development & Empowerment

True change is sustainable. We work at the root — building skills, infrastructure, and self-reliance in rural communities.

Our Rural Development program focuses on creating lasting improvements in villages around Haridwar. We run sanitation drives to promote hygiene awareness, skills training workshops for women and youth, and help connect rural families with government welfare schemes.

By training individuals in income-generating skills — tailoring, handicrafts, basic computer literacy, farming techniques — we help communities break the cycle of poverty through their own efforts.

Our Initiatives

  • Sanitation and hygiene promotion drives
  • Women's skills training (tailoring, handicrafts)
  • Youth vocational guidance sessions
  • Government scheme awareness and enrollment help
  • Village cleanliness and tree plantation campaigns
